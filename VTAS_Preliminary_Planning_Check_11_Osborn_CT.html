Process - Quick Guide

Getting your building project approved doesn’t have to be a hassle. Everyday Tiny Homes has partnered with the award-winning architectural studio VTAS to simplify the process and get your project moving faster. With extensive experience navigating complex approval pathways, VTAS ensures a smooth and efficient journey by cutting through red tape, saving you time and money, and providing expert advice tailored to your needs. This document outlines the three key stages of the Lodge approval process, so you’ll know what to expect at every step. As the design partner for The Lodge, VTAS’s friendly and knowledgeable team will be with you from start to finish, offering comprehensive support to get you building faster.


We send you a quote outlining the expected costs involved for your building approval. Simply sign and return this and pay the relevant fee to kick start your approvals process.


Your signed acceptance is our authorisation to proceed. Once we have this and payment, we will issue a notice to council that our certifier is engaged and we get your approval underway.

Stage 1 Quote and Engage



Get in touch with VTAS for a quote. Please provide your address and

a site plan of your property with

your preferred build location, send everything to approvals@vtas.com.au

Stage 2



We will issue you a request for information (RFI) which is a list of the items we need to complete the approval. Some of these items will need to come from other service providers eg: geotechnical engineers and builders for example.

Once you return all the items on the RFI we can issue the building approval. The documents will have a list of conditions and stamped constructions plans. Copies of the approval and plans are lodged with

council. You can now get on with your build.

Approval Process



We review your plans and location, if your intended use or site doesn’t

qualify as an ‘accepted development’

we refer to our town planners for a

development application. You will be advised of any additional applications and costs (and how to avoid them).


With the approval issued you can commence building work. You notify us when inspections are needed for framing and footings (an inspection schedule will be on the approval documentation).

Stage 3



You also need to notify us if changes are made to the approved plans during the construction process.

When you have finished construction, you book a final inspection with us.


Once the final inspection is completed and all required documentation submitted we will issue the final approval (a Form 21).

A copy of the Form 21 is issued to council for permanent record.
